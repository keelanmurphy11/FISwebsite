Fitness Is Simple

Fitness is simple... or at least it should be. Unfortunatly these days it is often way overcomplicated.
Social media and the endless number of articles online providing "fitness advice" only add to the confusuion around what it means to be fit and healthy. The reality of the situation is that the majority of the people providing this advice are more concerned with getting clicks and engagement rather than actually providing useful information to people.
This website aims to arm you with basic information on key areas of fitness including, muscle, nutrition and fat loss. Below is a really quick summary of the main topics covered:

Cardiorespiratory Fitness:

  • Cardiorespiratory fitness is one of the biggest indicators of lifespan.
  • Find a form of cardio you enjoy doing.
  • Most of your cardio can be easy e.g. a brisk walk or slow jog.
  • Incorporate some higher intensity cardio to challenge your heart.

Muscle:

  • Importance of muscle: Improves quality of life and independence as you age, helps with weight management, reduces risk of osteoporosis, and much more.
  • Everyone should be strength training, muscle mass begins to decrease after 30!
  • Strength training does not have to take up much time, with just two 30-minute sessions a week you can make significant progress.
  • Key considerations when strength training: perfect the technique, lift heavy, lift close to failure, rest at least one day between sessions, try to add weight or add a repitition each session.

Nutrition:

  • Balance is key.
  • Prioritsie Protein!
  • Carbs are not your enemy.
  • Make healthy eating enjoyable.

Fat loss:

  • Start slow, don't go 100% from the get go. Small changes compound over time creating big results.
  • Prioritise diet, not exercise.
  • Create a calorie deficit, burn more calories than you consume.
  • Don't strive for perfection, just work to be a slightly healthier version of yourself.
  • There is no such thing as "the best" diet. Find whatever works for you, dieting should be sustainable over the long run.
